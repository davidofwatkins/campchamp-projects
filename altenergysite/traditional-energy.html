Traditional Energies: Why Fossil Fuels are Bad

After years of industrialization, our world has become incredibly dependent upon fossil fuels to power our transportation, technology, warm water, and more. But, we are running dangeriously low on these fuels, and unless we find alternatives, our future may not be as bright as we would like to imagine it to be.

The process of converting natural fossil fuels into energy also taxes the environment by producing green gass emissions such as carbon dioxide, which are at least partially responsible for climate change and global warming. These gasses capture heat radiating from the earth within the atmosphere and, therefore, force temperatures to change (differently in different areas).
